üìã Individual Development Plan - Detailed Specifications

Function: Central hub for student's entire doctoral journey

Sections Include:

  • Student Profile: Name, NUID, email, start date, research area, department, primary/co-advisor
  • Research Approach: STEM/Humanities/Practice-Based (self-identified, not forced by department)
  • Year-by-Year Goals: Fall/Spring/Summer goals for Years 1-5
  • Milestone Planning: Target dates for qualifying exam, proposal defense, dissertation defense
  • Skills Development: Technical, methodological, professional skills to acquire
  • Career Planning: Evolving thoughts from exploration (Year 1-2) to specific plans (Year 4-5)
  • Documentation Archive: CV/resume history uploaded via check-ins

Integration Points:

  • Profile data auto-populates Compact student section
  • Goals displayed in Check-ins for reflection on progress
  • Milestone plans referenced in Annual Review
  • Research approach determines discipline-specific question adaptations

User Experience:

Students see IDP Dashboard showing: current goals, upcoming milestones, check-in history, annual review history, CV/resume archive. One-stop view of entire journey.

üî¨ Lab Manual - Detailed Specifications

Function: Faculty document expectations once, share with all advisees

Three Discipline-Specific Templates:

  • STEM/Computational: Code management, data management, computational resources, reproducibility, lab meetings, publications
  • Humanities/Social Science: Reading practices, qualitative methods, IRB, archival research, writing practices, publication culture
  • Practice-Based/Creative: Practice as research, critique culture, studio practices, exhibition/performance, documentation, materials

Common Sections All Templates:

  • Welcome & research philosophy
  • Communication preferences & availability
  • Lab culture & values
  • Collaboration & authorship expectations
  • Resources available (space, equipment, funding)
  • Professional development support
  • Diversity, equity, inclusion commitments

Benefits:

  • For Faculty: Write once, share with all advisees. Updates easier (modify once per year).
  • For Students: Clear expectations before even joining lab. Reference throughout relationship.
  • For Program: Transparency. New students see what they're joining.

ü§ù Advising Compact - Detailed Specifications

Function: Negotiated agreement between student and advisor establishing working relationship

Two-Part Structure:

Part 1: Student Section (pre-populated from IDP)

  • Working style & preferences
  • Career goals & aspirations
  • Support needs & concerns
  • Communication preferences

Part 2: Advisor Section (references Lab Manual)

  • Mentoring philosophy & approach
  • Availability & typical response times
  • Resources provided
  • Expectations & non-negotiables

Mutual Agreements Section:

  • Meeting Schedule: Frequency (weekly/bi-weekly/monthly), duration, format (in-person/virtual), standing time if applicable
  • Response Times: Email (24hr/48hr/week), document feedback (by type and length), urgent matters protocol
  • Communication Channels: Email for X, Slack for Y, in-person for Z
  • Milestone Preparation: When to start preparing for each milestone, readiness criteria, support needed
  • RA/TA Expectations: If funded, hours per week, balance with dissertation
  • Professional Development: Conference attendance, publication goals, teaching opportunities

Living Document:

Reviewed and updated annually during Annual Review process. Both parties discuss what's working, what needs adjustment. Changes signed off by both.

‚òÄÔ∏è Summer Check-in - Detailed Specifications

Function: Developmental reflection, not administrative reporting

Question Structure (15-20 minutes):

Section 1: Research Development

  • "How has your research question/focus evolved since [last check-in]?"
  • "What new connections or insights have emerged?"
  • "What's one thing you understand differently now?"
  • Discipline-specific prompts (all visible, student chooses relevant ones)

Section 2: Integration Questions

  • [STEM]: "How are theoretical concepts shaping implementation?"
  • [Humanities]: "How is fieldwork/archival work challenging your framework?"
  • [Practice]: "How is your creative practice generating new knowledge?"

Section 3: Developmental Thinking

  • "What skills are you developing that you didn't anticipate?"
  • "What challenges are you working through?"
  • "Where do you feel stuck? (It's okay - this is part of the process)"

Section 4: Career Orientation (scaffolded by year)

  • Year 1-2: "What work environments are you curious about?"
  • Year 3-4: "How does your research connect to potential careers?"
  • Year 4-5: "What specific next steps post-graduation?"

Section 5: Support & Resources

  • "What support would be most helpful right now?"
  • "Are you meeting with advisors as planned in compact?"
  • "What resources do you need access to?"

Section 6: CV/Resume Upload (REQUIRED)

  • Upload current CV or Resume
  • Max 52MB file size
  • Stored in personal archive

Framing:

"This check-in is for YOU, not just for the record. Take time to reflect on your growth as a researcher, artist, and scholar."

üçÇ Fall Check-in - Detailed Specifications

Function: Same as Summer check-in + forward planning for spring

Same Structure as Summer, Plus:

  • Review of Last Annual Review: "What feedback did you receive in April? What progress have you made on that feedback?"
  • Spring Planning: "Looking ahead to spring semester, what needs to happen?" (courses, milestones, research activities)
  • CV/Resume: Optional upload (vs. required in summer)

All other sections identical to Summer check-in: research development, integration questions, developmental thinking, career orientation, support needs.

üìä Annual Review (360¬∞) - Detailed Specifications

Function: Comprehensive mutual assessment generating discussion guide

Four Components:

Part 1: Student Self-Assessment

  • Research progress toward milestones
  • Skills developed
  • Publications, presentations, creative works
  • Challenges encountered and how addressed
  • Growth areas
  • Career planning progress
  • Goals for next year

Part 2: Faculty Assessment of Student

  • Research progress assessment
  • Skills development observed
  • Areas of strength
  • Areas for growth
  • Recommendations for student
  • CONFIDENTIAL SECTION (Academic Director only):
    • Probation consideration (yes/no + rationale)
    • Dismissal consideration (yes/no + rationale)
    • Serious concerns requiring intervention
    • Confidential notes for Academic Director

Part 3: Student Feedback on Advising (NEW - makes it 360¬∞)

  • Communication and accessibility
  • Quality of guidance received
  • What's working well in the relationship
  • What could improve
  • Requests for changes or adjustments

Part 4: Faculty Self-Reflection (NEW - makes it 360¬∞)

  • What's working well in this advising relationship
  • Areas where I could support student better
  • Commitments I'm making for the year ahead
  • Resources or support I need to be a better advisor

Process Flow:

  1. Both student and faculty complete their parts independently
  2. Academic Director reviews confidential flags (probation/dismissal)
  3. System generates Discussion Guide highlighting:
    • Areas of alignment
    • Areas of disconnect
    • Action items for both parties
    • Compact updates needed
  4. Student and advisor meet to discuss using guide
  5. Both sign off on discussion outcomes
  6. Compact updated if needed
  7. Academic Director receives final signed review

Cultural Shift:

This is a move from one-directional evaluation (faculty assess student) to developmental conversation (mutual feedback for mutual growth). Faculty self-reflection demonstrates openness to improving mentoring.

Data Integration Flow

IDP ‚Üí Compact

Student profile, research approach, goals, career aspirations auto-populate Compact student section

Lab Manual ‚Üí Compact

Faculty expectations, resources, communication norms referenced when creating Compact advisor section

IDP ‚Üí Check-ins

Current goals displayed in check-ins for reflection on progress. Check-ins ask: "Your IDP says your goal is X. How's that going?"

Compact ‚Üí Check-ins

Agreed-upon meeting schedule checked: "Your compact says you meet weekly. Did this happen consistently?"

Check-ins ‚Üí Annual Review

Check-in responses summarized in Annual Review: "In August you said X. In December you said Y. Progress?"

Annual Review ‚Üí Next Annual Review

Previous year's feedback referenced: "Last year's review identified Z as growth area. Progress this year?"

Annual Review ‚Üí Compact Update

If review identifies needed changes (meeting frequency, communication, etc.), Compact is updated and re-signed
